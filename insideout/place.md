# Place

This file holds an open question that belongs to no other file in this repo: **where is the natural place of the author of OpenLight in the life of InsideOut?** The author sits on InsideOut's board and carries this research project at the same time. The two are not separate lives, but the shape of how they are one life is not yet settled. This file is where that shape is being listened for.

Written inside-out, as the other seed files are. The answer, if there is one, is uncovered, not designed. Values and questions come first. Proposals wait until they feel inevitable, not clever. The point of the file is not to arrive somewhere — it is to hold the search honestly enough that any session picking it up can continue the listening without starting over.

---

## Why the file exists

A session asking where the author fits in the VP can answer from gut feeling alone, and drift. It can also answer from a single conversation's context, and forget. Neither is enough for a question that is going to live across months and across sessions. The file is the attempt to make the search *session-agnostic* — to hold what is already seen, what is only felt, and what is still open, in a place a future session can return to without starting over.

If a future session reads this file and feels it is incomplete, the incompleteness is not a failure. It is an invitation. Add what has been seen since. Mark what has moved from thought to settled, and what has moved the other way. Do not resolve open angles to feel productive. The openness is part of the instrument.

---

## The question, plainly

The author of OpenLight is on the board of InsideOut. Where does the research work — the substrate, the system, the long arc of what OpenLight is trying to uncover — sit in the life of an operating humanitarian organization with programs, grants, a verksamhetsplan, and real people in rooms?

Two tempting answers are held at arm's length, because naming them is how they stop leading.

**"The system makes communications materials."** The research substrate can plausibly hold InsideOut's knowledge and generate a website, program descriptions, grant language, even parts of the VP itself. The work would be real. The funding could come from communications budgets inside project grants, or from direct communications grants. This is a concrete path, and it is not wrong — but treating it as the answer flattens the research into a production function, and rests on a grant shape the author has not yet explored. It is one possible shape among several, not a resolution. The assumption should not lead.

**"The system is unrelated to the VP."** Equally tempting, and equally incomplete. The research is alive in the same person who sits on the board; the inside-out claim in both projects is literally the same claim in two dialects; and the author's hands will do both kinds of work whether the VP names it or not. Saying the two are separate is convenient but untrue to what is already the case.

The right answer is somewhere the tempting ones are not.

## The current gut feeling — held as hypothesis

When the question is left to sit, a shape has started to appear. It is not yet proven, and it should not be spoken as settled. Named here honestly so a future session can test it or let it go:

*The author's natural place may be a research and infrastructure layer underneath the operating programs — not a parallel production function bolted on the side, but a living ground the programs can draw from and contribute back to.*

In this shape, InsideOut's programs are the operating work. They meet people in rooms. They are where the values become visible. As of the 2026 VP: Nova Klubb, Steady Mind, and the incoming Steady Steps under Next Generation; Healing Beyond Fear, The Shift, Creator Program, and Conscious Leadership under Personal Evolution; Wisdom Wheels under Connected Society. The research and infrastructure layer is something the author keeps alive for and with them: a place where participant voices, program moments, measurement data, voice-level knowledge, grant drafts, and method documentation all live as one body, rather than as separate artifacts each maintained in its own corner.

What radiates from such a layer is then not a line of "communications deliverables." A website grows out of it. Grant language grows out of it. Apps for Wisdom Wheels or the next program grow out of it. The VP itself becomes readable from it and writable into it. These are not products the layer *makes* — they are faces the layer *has*, because it is alive.

Read this way, the author's place is not "the person who handles communications for the board." It is the person who keeps a research and infrastructure layer alive underneath and around the operating work, and lets the surfaces everyone sees — website, apps, reports, the VP — emerge from that aliveness rather than be assembled separately.

This is a feeling, not a plan. It has the quality of *inevitability* when the author holds it, which is a signal that matters, but a signal is not proof. If it turns out to be forced once tested against real grant shapes, real board capacity, and real program needs, the file should say so and the shape should move.

### The VP is already reaching for this shape

Worth noting plainly, because it changes the weight of the hypothesis: the language the research-and-infrastructure-layer idea needs is already in the VP, in InsideOut's own institutional Swedish, arrived at without any outside frame.

§8 *Långsiktig inriktning* describes InsideOut as an **innovationsaktör** whose role is to develop methods and environments. The same section names the long-term ambition as *"att över tid etablera InsideOut som en plattform där praktiskt arbete, metodutveckling och samverkan integreras"* — a platform where practical work, method development, and collaboration are integrated. It also uses the word **regenerativa** natively: *"bidra till utvecklingen av mer sammanhängande och regenerativa arbetssätt i samhället."*

§2 names the goal of 2026 as *"ett multidimensionellt center för mänsklig utveckling och social hållbarhet"* — a multidimensional center. The root `inside.md` of this repo has an entire vision section titled "Multidimensional, never sliced." The same word, reached for in two different projects from two different directions.

§9 names **"etablera strukturen för InsideOut Center"** as one of 2026's overarching goals. There is already a structural object being built, named in the VP, that the research and infrastructure layer could belong to — or, more openly, that the author's work might naturally help take shape from the inside.

None of this is borrowed from anywhere. It is what InsideOut is already saying about itself. The hypothesis above is not an external frame — it is a name for something the VP is already reaching for in its own voice. That strengthens the gut feeling without resolving it. The test is still *inevitability*, and the inevitability has to survive actual attempts to stand on it before it counts as settled.

## Why this does not reshape InsideOut

One risk must be named plainly, because without it the file drifts: it is easy for a research project carrying its own vocabulary to quietly reframe the organization it is serving. That would be a kind of overwriting — values imported from one inside into another inside, against the grain of the work already alive.

The values this file answers to are the values in `core.md`. Healing begins inside. A person is already whole. A person is a creator. Pain is a doorway. Inside and outside are one motion. These are the inside of InsideOut, and any place the author takes in the VP must serve *them*, not refine them into a different register.

The research work has its own register — the root `inside.md` in this repo holds it. Both registers are real. The translation between them is the actual work. A sentence, a program shape, or a piece of infrastructure belongs here if it answers to the five values above, *in the register the room asks for*. Nothing else.

## Open angles — left explicitly open

A list of things this file is not resolving. Some will resolve as real work happens. Some will remain open for a long time. None should be closed because an answer is tidy.

**Funding shapes.** *Updated April 2026.* The landscape has been explored — see `grant-landscape.md` for the full map. The patient look has been taken. What it showed: the work has two faces that funders recognize. The social face — method development, psychosocial measurement, digital tools for programs — is legible to Arvsfonden, Forte, ESF+, IOP structures, Erasmus+. The technology face — an open-source computing substrate, agent-native infrastructure, digital public goods — is legible to NLnet, Shuttleworth, EIC Pathfinder, Sovereign Tech Fund. Both faces tell the same truth. No single grant carries the whole; the layer is funded by showing each face in the register the funder asks for. This is, notably, the same two-register reality `ground.md` describes as the nature of InsideOut's voice. The funding shapes are real and multiple. This angle has moved from open to seen — not resolved, but no longer unexplored.

**Measurement as a living field.** InsideOut's measurement model is clear and defensible — the thresholds and methods sit in VP §9 *Mål och uppföljning*: participant registration, attendance data, self-rating surveys before and after, observations from program leaders, feedback from participants and partners. Concrete per-area targets (70% of children showing improvement in concentration, social interaction, emotional regulation; 75% of participants reporting increased self-insight and stress handling; 80% of Wisdom Wheels participants reporting a stronger sense of belonging). The open question is whether a research and infrastructure layer could hold this alongside the things that never make it into a number: a child's afternoon at Nova Klubb, a sentence from a Healing Beyond Fear session, a moment in a Wisdom Wheels circle. Not as a replacement for the instruments, as the environment the numbers live inside — so the numbers stay connected to the people they come from, and what the programs see but cannot yet count has a place to sit. The VP is explicit that measurement already feeds method development and funding cases; a living field would deepen that loop, not alter its direction.

**InsideOut Center as a structural home, or not.** VP §9 names *"etablera strukturen för InsideOut Center"* as one of 2026's overarching goals. The Center is a structural object being built this year, and the research and infrastructure layer may turn out to belong inside it — as its knowledge spine, or as the place where methods and participant voices are integrated across programs. It may also turn out that Center and research layer are two different things that touch. The open question is what shape the Center wants to take, and whether the author's work is part of its structure, adjacent to it, or feeding into it from outside. Held as: the Center is a concrete 2026 goal, not yet defined in detail, and the author's place question and the Center's shape question may end up being the same question.

**Wisdom Wheels and the next program as concrete surfaces.** These will need digital tools regardless of the research. They are the nearest places where the research and operating work could meet in something a participant can open on a phone. The natural shape of that meeting has not been drawn yet — whether an app is something the substrate generates, something it serves, or something entirely separate that only borrows from it, is open. But they are where abstractions will be forced to become real fastest.

**Letting people appear, structurally.** The VP review noted that §3 describes people by what is done to them rather than letting them appear. A research and infrastructure layer that carries participant voices and program moments as first-class content would address this not editorially, as a writing pass, but structurally, as something the substrate does by its nature. Whether that is the right way in or a distraction is open. The pull of it is that it answers something the existing review already flagged as missing.

**Shapes not yet seen.** The system's potential is larger than the angles above name. Foreclosing early is the failure mode. This angle is kept in the file on purpose, as a reminder that the list is not the territory. A future session that discovers a shape not listed above should add it, not force it into one of the existing headings.

## What must not be lost

- **Do not import language from outside.** Anything useful from a neighbouring project, a framework, or a book must be translated into the native voice of this repo before it belongs here. Borrowed vocabulary is a quiet way of overwriting the inside.
- **Do not rush to mechanism.** Grant type, digital work commitment, funding shape, tool choice — all of these come *after* the place is clear enough to stand on. Mechanism-first is the wrong order and produces motion without direction.
- **Do not collapse hypothesis into claim.** The gut feeling above is a feeling. Spoken as a decision, it taints the work. Hold the tone.
- **Do not treat the two hats as a conflict to resolve.** The author being both the one on the board and the one who wrote OpenLight is not a problem. It is the condition. The file is trying to notice what that condition already is, not pick one hat and put the other down.
- **Do not close this file prematurely.** When the place is clear enough to act on, act. Do not mistake the first answer that feels defensible for the natural one. The test is still *inevitability*, and the file should not be closed in its absence.

## How a future session picks this up

Read the seed files in order — `inside.md`, `core.md`, `ground.md`, `voices.md` — then this file. The other seed files hold the inside of InsideOut itself. This file holds a question about the author's relationship to it. The order matters: the question is only askable once the inside is held.

When you arrive here:

1. Hold the gut feeling lightly. Ask whether it still feels inevitable or whether it has started to feel clever. Either answer is load-bearing.
2. Look at what has happened since the file was last touched — grant conversations, program shifts, the next app, a new voice in the room, something the author has seen that this file does not yet know — and ask which open angles have moved.
3. Add what has been seen. Mark what has shifted between thought and settled. Do not delete angles just to feel tidy.
4. [x] If the file stops being the right shape for the question, rewrite it. The file is a seed, not a specification. Its job is to be alive, not to be preserved.

## A closing note

This is not a plan. It is the ground a plan can stand on, once the ground is firm enough to hold one. If a future session feels the pull to turn it into a plan before the inside is settled, that pull is exactly what this file was written to resist. Sit with the question a little longer. The shape is already there. The work is to notice it.

## April 2026 — what has moved

The grant landscape has been mapped from scratch (`grant-landscape.md`). The funding shapes are real and multiple — both the social face (method development, psychosocial measurement) and the technology face (open-source substrate, agent-native computing) are recognized by funders independently.

What also became clear: the author's posture is not to take on obligations. The research is the author's own. It may feed InsideOut, it may feed many things. The work will show itself. The VP may not need additions to name this — the author sits on the board, carries research that may feed into InsideOut's evolution, and that does not require institutional permission or a funding line to exist.

The question this file holds has not been answered. It has been clarified: the question is not "what role should the VP name" but "what is the author actually going to do this year, and does the VP need to know." That is still open, and the openness is honest.
