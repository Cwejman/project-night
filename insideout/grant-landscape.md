# Grant Landscape

This file maps the grant landscape for two things that are one thing: InsideOut's research-and-infrastructure layer, and the OpenLight substrate that is the technology underneath it. Method development. Digital infrastructure. A new computing substrate where knowledge, agents, and interfaces live as one field. The person building and maintaining it.

Assembled from scratch in April 2026 across nine research axes. Each entry carries an honest fit assessment.

---

## Two faces of one work

The substrate is a genuine technology innovation — a self-describing field with one primitive (chunks + placements + scopes), agent-native computation, lossless history, and interfaces derived from type contracts. Its first application is InsideOut's programs. But the substrate is general-purpose, open-source, and architecturally novel.

This means the work has two faces in the grant landscape:

**The social face.** Method development for psychosocial programs, digital tools for war-displaced families, measurement innovation, knowledge infrastructure for a humanitarian nonprofit. This face speaks to: Arvsfonden, Forte, ESF+, IOPs, Erasmus+, EPIM, Nordic funds.

**The technology face.** An open-source computing substrate, a new approach to how knowledge and AI agents relate, digital public infrastructure, agent-native computing. This face speaks to: NLnet, Sovereign Tech Fund, Shuttleworth, EIC Pathfinder, Internetstiftelsen, FLOSS/fund.

Both faces tell the same truth. The strategy is to show each face to the funders who recognize it.

## Tier 1 — primary paths

The grants and structures with the strongest fit. Each is actionable in 2026.

### NLnet — NGI Zero Commons Fund

**What:** EU-funded grants for open-source internet commons — "the entire technology spectrum, from libre silicon to middleware, from P2P infrastructure to convenient end user applications." Distributed by NLnet Foundation (Netherlands) under the EU's Next Generation Internet programme.

**Scale:** 5,000–50,000 EUR per proposal, scalable upward. Maximum 500,000 EUR per recipient over the fund's lifetime. Total pot: 21.6 million EUR to be awarded by 2027.

**Deadline:** June 1, 2026 (13th call). Calls run every two months.

**Eligibility:** Any organization or individual, anywhere. No geographic restriction. Results must be released under open-source licenses.

**Application:** Simple online form at nlnet.nl/propose. Lightweight compared to most EU funding.

**Why it fits:** NLnet has recently funded projects directly analogous to OpenLight — SHACL-based UI generation from data shapes, vector similarity search for graph databases, NextGraph (decentralized semantic data repositories). A self-describing computing substrate with a novel primitive, agent-native architecture, and lossless history is exactly what this fund exists for. NLnet is designed for individual developers and small teams. The application is short and the approval rate for good projects is reasonable.

**What to do:** Apply by June 1. This is the single highest-probability match for the technology face.

### Shuttleworth Foundation Fellowship

**What:** Individual fellowships for people implementing innovative ideas for social change, with openness as the key requirement. The foundation works at the intersection of technology, knowledge, and learning.

**Scale:** One-year fellowship (renewable). Salary equivalent plus $275,000/year in project funding. The foundation matches the fellow's personal investment at least 10:1. Total package roughly $300–400K+ per year.

**Deadline:** May 1, 2026 (for September 2026 intake). Approximately 1% of applicants selected.

**Application:** Form, 5-minute video, resume.

**Why it fits:** An open-source computing substrate, first deployed for a war-displaced-families nonprofit, hits multiple Shuttleworth sweet spots — openness, technology, knowledge systems, social impact. The fellowship is to the *person*, not the organization, which works well for a solo builder. The key question is whether the concept is concrete enough to demonstrate near-term social change, not just technical novelty. The InsideOut pilot answers that.

**What to do:** Prepare application and video by May 1. High upside, competitive but worth the attempt.

### Allmänna Arvsfonden — projektstöd

**What:** Method development or new activities serving children (0–11), youth (12–25), or people with disabilities. Explicitly describes projektstöd as funding to "develop a method or activity." The method must be documented in a method guide for spread to other actors.

**Scale:** No upper budget limit. Funds 100% of project costs. Real-world examples from 2025: 2.7 MSEK, 5.9 MSEK, 6.2 MSEK. Total fund disbursement in 2024 was 846 MSEK. Duration: 1, 2, or 3 years.

**Application:** Rolling — no deadlines. Processing 4–6 months. Phone consultation Tuesdays and Thursdays 9–11. ~25% approval rate.

**Eligibility:** Ideell förening active for at least 1 year.

**Why it fits:** Nova Klubb (320 children) places InsideOut in the target group. Method development is what the fund is for. Digital tools can be framed as the documentation and spread mechanism. A researcher/method-developer salary can be included.

**Challenge:** Arvsfonden expects transferable methods. The digital infrastructure must be framed concretely — a documentation platform holding session guides, measurement tools, participant stories — not as "a computing substrate." The participation criterion means children and families must be involved in shaping the project.

**What to do:** Apply for a 3-year projektstöd, 3–6 MSEK. Social face. Frame the substrate as the method's documentation, measurement, and spread mechanism.

### IOP with Göteborgs Stad

**What:** An Idéburet Offentligt Partnerskap — a partnership agreement where both parties contribute toward a shared societal goal. Designed for situations where "there are no ready-made solutions or services that can be purchased."

**The proof case:** Ericastiftelsen holds an IOP with Region Stockholm since 2018 specifically for method development of psychosocial-pedagogical support for children with complex needs. Their IOP explicitly includes method development, documentation, evaluation, and implementation support. This proves IOPs can carry method development and infrastructure work.

**Scale:** Negotiated per partnership. Can include salary costs, infrastructure, and development work.

**Contact:** The team for samverkan med civilsamhället at socialutveckling.goteborg.se.

**What to do:** Approach with a proposal modeled on Ericastiftelsen. Social face.

---

### Forte — via university partnership

**What:** Sweden's research council for health, working life, and welfare. ~900 MSEK annually. "Collaboration in Practice for Mental Health" — up to 5 MSEK per project, 4-year duration, ~150 MSEK total. Requires a doctoral-level PI.

**Entry point:** Forte planning grants, up to 700 kSEK for one year, designed to build researcher-organization partnerships.

**The academic door:** GU's Department of Social Work has RIO (Referensgruppen för Idéburna Organisationer) — a formal collaboration platform between the department and nonprofits. This is the door to walk through.

**What to do:** Contact RIO. Find a researcher. Apply jointly for a planning grant. Then the next major project call.

### ESF+ 25-056 — Social Innovation

**What:** EU Social Fund: "Socialt innovativa medel och mål för att möta samhällsutmaningar." Typical budgets 2–10 MSEK over ~29 months.

**Deadline:** September 3, 2026.

**Eligibility:** Ideella föreningar explicitly eligible.

**Support:** Monthly learning sessions — April 15, May 13, June 17, 2026 (Mötesplats Social Innovation, Högskolan i Borås).

**Challenge:** Significant administrative overhead. Co-financing required.

**What to do:** Attend the April 15 session. Confirm requirements. Social face.

## Tier 2 — strong fit, requires preparation

### EIC Pathfinder Challenges — "DeepRAP"

**What:** European Innovation Council: "Deep Reasoning, Abstraction & Planning towards trustworthy Cognitive AI Systems." Up to 4 million EUR per project, 100% funding, lump sum.

**Deadline:** October 28, 2026. Single applicants or 2-partner consortia allowed for Challenges.

**Why it fits:** OpenLight is the substrate that gives cognitive AI systems identity, traceability, structured knowledge, and a self-describing environment. The "trustworthy" framing maps directly to the substrate's built-in traceability — every mutation traceable to dispatch, invocable, session. Technology face.

**Challenge:** The challenge text needs close reading. A 2-partner consortium is manageable but needs finding.

**What to do:** Read the full challenge description. Find one partner (university or research institute). If the fit holds, apply.

### Internetstiftelsen (Swedish Internet Foundation)

**What:** Funds non-commercial projects that benefit internet development in Sweden. Since 2004: ~400 projects, ~75 MSEK total. Two application rounds per year.

**Eligibility:** Must have Swedish org number. An ideell förening qualifies.

**Why it fits:** An open-source computing substrate built to advance how the internet works — self-describing, agent-native, lossless — is directly within scope. Technology face, Swedish.

**What to do:** Contact Internetstiftelsen to confirm Internetfonden is still running active calls in 2026.

### Sovereign Tech Fund (Germany)

**What:** Open-source infrastructure grants. Awards start at 50,000 EUR, no fixed upper limit. Rolling applications. Has invested 23+ million EUR in open-source projects.

**Why it fits:** Open-source computing substrate as foundational infrastructure. Technology face.

**Challenge:** STF tends to fund established, widely-adopted infrastructure (curl, OpenSSL). A novel substrate without broad adoption is a harder sell. Worth applying once there's more to show.

### EPIM (European Programme for Integration and Migration)

25 private foundations (including Robert Bosch Stiftung, King Baudouin Foundation) funding civil society on migration. Average EUR 50,000/year. ~150 applications per year. Social face. Monitor epim.info.

### Stenastiftelsen (Sten A Olssons Stiftelse)

Gothenburg-based. Funds research, culture, integration/inclusion. 560 MSEK historical giving. Two-stage: 1–2 page pre-proposal first. No fixed deadlines. Max 3 years. Social face. Email: info@stenastiftelsen.se.

### Skandia Idéer för Livet

37 years of method development for preventive work with children and youth. 260+ MSEK to 4,600+ projects. Explicitly funds measurement and methodology innovation. Social face. Contact directly.

### Erasmus+ KA2 — Small-Scale Partnership

EUR 30,000–60,000. Minimum 2 organizations from 2 countries. Youth field via MUCF. Autumn deadline ~October 1, 2026. The lowest-barrier EU grant. Social face. Need one cross-border partner.

### Vinnova — Impact Innovation (Accelerationspotten)

Up to 800,000 SEK. Explicitly welcomes civil society actors. Within Impact Innovation framework. Non-economic organizations can get up to 100% funding. Watch for fall 2026 round. Hybrid face.

### Nordiska ministerrådet — Prosperous Future

EUR 45,000–65,000 for Nordic-Baltic civil society cooperation. Includes Ukraine partnerships. 2026 deadline has passed. Monitor for next round. Social face.

### FLOSS/fund

$10,000–$100,000. Publish a `funding.json` manifest in the repo. Applications are perpetual. $1M/year budget. Technology face. Zero effort to start.

### Anthropic — Claude for Nonprofits

Up to 75% off Team and Enterprise plans. InsideOut as a Swedish nonprofit should qualify. Not project funding but immediate operational benefit for the pilot. Register at claude.com/solutions/nonprofits.

## Tier 3 — future positioning

### EIC Pathfinder Open

Up to 4 million EUR, 100% funding, TRL 1–4, no thematic restriction. Requires 3 partners from 3 countries. May 2026 deadline likely too close. Prepare for 2027 round.

### Erasmus+ KA2 — full Cooperation Partnership

EUR 120,000–400,000. 3 organizations, 3 countries. March 2027 deadline. Build toward after completing a Small-Scale Partnership.

### AMIF (Asyl-, migrations- och integrationsfonden)

EU asylum/migration/integration fund. Up to 75% of costs. Monitor Migrationsverket's utlysningar page. Social face.

### Horizon Europe Cluster 2

"Culture, Creativity and Inclusive Society." Up to EUR 3–4 million. Consortium partner with university coordinating. Register for PIC number now.

### Horizon Europe Cluster 4

"Digital, Industry, Space." Innovative AI methods, data, robotics. EUR 6 million per project. Large consortia required. Register on the Cluster 4 matchmaking platform.

### Marianne och Marcus Wallenbergs Stiftelse

Social science research, 4–10 MSEK over 3–5 years. Universities only. Same academic path as Forte.

### WASP (Wallenberg AI, Autonomous Systems and Software Program)

Sweden's largest research program. Exclusively for partner university researchers. If a Chalmers or GU professor is interested in OpenLight as a research platform, WASP funding becomes possible.

### Postkodstiftelsen 2027

Requires Giva Sverige membership. Average ~2 MSEK. Excludes research/tech-development-primary projects. Social face with careful framing.

### UNHCR Refugee-led Innovation Fund

Up to USD 45,000. Must be refugee-led. Conditional on leadership composition.

### DPGA (Digital Public Goods Alliance)

Registration is free and worth doing for visibility. The ecosystem is oriented toward developing-country infrastructure deployment. Register when the project is more mature.

## Not worth pursuing

- **Tillväxtverket** — business growth and SME competitiveness.
- **Knut och Alice Wallenbergs Stiftelse** — natural sciences, technology, medicine only.
- **Familjen Kamprads Stiftelse** — elderly focus, Småland region.
- **Crafoordska stiftelsen** — Skåne region only.
- **EEA/Norway Grants** — Sweden not eligible as beneficiary.
- **MUCF grants** — operational/activity, not innovation.
- **Almi** — almost entirely company-only. Individual verification grant (25k SEK) is the only crack.
- **Gothenburg incubators (GU Ventures, Chalmers Ventures)** — assume company formation and equity.
- **Open Technology Fund** — internet freedom specifically, wrong framing.
- **Ford / Sloan Foundations** — invitation-only, US institutional focus.
- **Chan Zuckerberg Initiative** — biological/biomedical research tools only.
- **Prototype Fund (Germany)** — requires German residency.
- **VGR digitaliseringscheckar** — SME-only.

## Structural question: the companion AB

Almost everything in the Swedish startup ecosystem — Almi, VGR checks, Vinnova Innovativa Startups, incubators — requires an aktiebolag (AB). An ideell f��rening building open-source infrastructure is a poor fit for these programs. Many European open-source projects operate with a dual structure: a nonprofit holding the vision and the code, a small AB doing consulting, support, or integration work. If the Swedish ecosystem keeps closing gates to the förening, forming a companion AB is the structural answer. This is a decision to hold, not rush.

## How a future session picks this up

This file is a landscape, not a plan. It maps what exists and where the fit is. A future session should:

1. Check which actions have been taken and what responses came back.
2. Update entries as new information arrives — calls open and close, relationships form, framings get tested in real conversations.
3. Remove dead ends. Add what emerges.
4. Watch for the convergence point: when enough paths have been explored that the author's role in the VP can be named concretely, grounded in real grant shapes rather than hypothesis.

The hypothesis in `place.md` — the author as a research-and-infrastructure layer underneath the operating programs — is strengthened by this landscape. Both faces of the work are recognized by funders. The technology face (NLnet, Shuttleworth, EIC, Sovereign Tech Fund) sees a novel open-source computing substrate. The social face (Arvsfonden, Forte, IOP, ESF+) sees method development and digital infrastructure for psychosocial programs. Both are the same work, described in the register each funder asks for.

What remains is to test the framings in real conversations with real funders, and to let what comes back reshape the hypothesis or confirm it.
