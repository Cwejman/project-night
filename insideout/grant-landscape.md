# Grant Landscape

Assembled April 2026. A map of funding shapes for the author's research and for InsideOut's broader needs. Not a plan — a reference for when a shape feels right.

The author's posture: freedom to research without heavy obligations. The work will show itself. What follows is filtered through that.

---

## Matches — free research, light obligations

### Shuttleworth Foundation Fellowship

Fellowship to the *person*. ~$300–400K/year, renewable. Salary equivalent plus $275K/year in project funding. The obligation is openness — what you make must be open-source. Not "deliver X by Y." The foundation funds people at the intersection of technology, knowledge, and social change.

Deadline: **May 1, 2026.** Form, 5-minute video, resume. ~1% acceptance rate.

Why it fits: open-source computing substrate + first application with war-displaced families + person-level fellowship. High upside.

### NLnet — NGI Zero Commons Fund

5,000–50,000 EUR for open-source internet commons. You propose milestones yourself — the deliverable is open-source code, which is what you're building anyway. Light reporting. Simple online form.

Deadline: **June 1, 2026.** Calls every two months. Scalable to 500K EUR over the fund's lifetime.

Why it fits: NLnet funds projects directly analogous to OpenLight — novel databases, semantic infrastructure, decentralized knowledge systems. Designed for individual developers and small teams. Highest-probability match.

### FLOSS/fund

$10,000–$100,000. Publish a `funding.json` manifest in the repo. Perpetual — no deadline. No obligations beyond having the file. $1M/year budget.

### GitHub Sponsors / Open Collective

Community funding. No obligations. Scales with visibility. Set up now, accumulates over time.

### Anthropic — Economic Futures Program

$10–50K + $5K API credits. Research grants for empirical research on AI's impacts. Rolling applications. Small but free.

### Anthropic — Claude for Nonprofits

75% off Team/Enterprise plans. InsideOut qualifies. Immediate operational benefit. Register at claude.com/solutions/nonprofits.

## InsideOut paths — if the organization wants to pursue them

These carry obligations — deliverables, reporting, target groups, timelines. They are not the author's personal research funding. They are paths InsideOut as an organization could take, with or without the author's substrate. Kept here as reference.

**Allmänna Arvsfonden** — method development for children/youth. 3–6 MSEK, 3 years, rolling. Must produce transferable methods. Social face.

**IOP with Göteborgs Stad** — partnership for method development. Ericastiftelsen precedent proves the shape works. Contact socialutveckling.goteborg.se.

**Forte via GU** — practice-based research. Needs a doctoral PI at university. Entry: RIO at GU Department of Social Work. Planning grants 700 kSEK.

**ESF+ Social Innovation** — 2–10 MSEK. Deadline September 3, 2026. Heavy admin. Learning sessions April–June.

**Erasmus+ Small-Scale** — EUR 30–60K with one cross-border partner. Autumn 2026 via MUCF.

**Skandia Idéer för Livet** — method/measurement innovation for children/youth. Contact directly.

**Stenastiftelsen** — Gothenburg-based, integration/inclusion. Pre-proposal, no fixed deadline.

**EPIM** — 25 foundations funding migration work. EUR ~50K/year. Monitor epim.info.
