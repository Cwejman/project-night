# Research — nourishment for the communications framework

This directory holds reference material gathered from outside InsideOut: verksamhetsplaner from comparable Swedish and international NGOs, and communication-strategy material — practical guides, academic work, and one load-bearing ethical code. The point is not to copy. The point is to have enough nourishment in the room that when the work of articulating InsideOut's own source begins, it is grounded in how others have actually written themselves onto the page, and where the form helps or gets in the way.

The near-term goal this serves is named in `seed/insideout.md` under *What is still being found → The question of voice*: the real work is not which register to pick, but grounding the source until both the grant register and the alive register flow from the same place without betraying each other. These materials are a mirror held up to that question — each one shows a shape another organization chose when it faced its own version of it.

## How to use this

Read inward. For each document, the question is not *what does this say* but *what was this organization carrying on the inside when it wrote this, and does any of it ring true here?* Borrow structures only when they feel inevitable; when they feel clever, put them down.

The documents below are ordered by how close they sit to InsideOut's actual shape. Closest first.

## verksamhetsplaner/

Swedish NGOs (and a few international ones) writing themselves into institutional form. All are downloaded as PDFs with `.txt` extracts alongside for quick reading and search.

### hug-bym-research-evaluation-2023-2025
*Better You&Me — Research & Evaluation Report*, December 2025. English, 127 pages, ISBN 978-91-990859-0-6. Published under Help Ukraine Gothenburg, written by the people who are now InsideOut.
**Not a peer reference — InsideOut's own prior voice.** Better You&Me is the work the InsideOut team did during the HUG phase, before spinning out as its own organization. That makes this document load-bearing in a specific way: it is the closest available answer to *what does InsideOut already sound like when it writes institutionally about its own work*. Contains theoretical framework, programme design, methodology, demographics, a "Layers of Trauma" model, a "Pathway Summary," and policy recommendations. Read first, and read as InsideOut's own register from a year ago — a starting point to carry forward and refine, not an external voice to learn from.

### hug-annual-report-2024
*Help Ukraine Gothenburg.* Verksamhetsberättelse 2024. Swedish.
The parent organization InsideOut spun out of. Documents the split between Ukraine-side humanitarian work (97 rescue vehicles, 40 MSEK medical aid, 5000 tourniquets) and Sweden-side integration and psychosocial work (2700 participants, 98% recommend rate) — the work InsideOut has now carried forward. The original PDF is scanned; the `.txt` is OCR and noisy, but the quantitative structure and framing are legible. Useful for understanding the institutional context InsideOut has emerged from and the dual posture (humanitarian ↔ integration) that the split preserved.

### roda-korset-halsa-vard-arsbok-2025
*Svenska Röda Korset, avdelning Hälsa och vård.* 40-årsjubileumsårsbok 2025.
The benchmark for how a Swedish organization describes the interior of traumatized displaced people in a mature institutional register without pathologizing them. The five Röda Korset treatment centres for war-injured and tortured are directly adjacent to InsideOut's territory, and the book contains 128 occurrences of *trauma / tortyr / PTSD / anknytning / flykt* — vocabulary used with care rather than clinical distance. For any dignity-register revision of §1 and §3 of the VP, this is the primary vocabulary source.

### roda-korset-halsa-vard-arsbok-2021
*Svenska Röda Korset, avdelning Hälsa och vård.* Årsbok 2021 — *Tortyren, kriget och flykten.*
Same series, four years earlier. Kept as a second data point to triangulate whether the register is stable over time (it is) and to check vocabulary drift. Useful mainly as confirmation that the 2025 voice is not a one-off.

### transkulturellt-centrum-psykosocialt-stod-faktablad
*Region Stockholm, Transkulturellt Centrum.* Faktablad — psykosocialt stöd och behandling för människor som flytt.
Short and dense. Explicitly names the gap between *psykosocialt stöd* as lay-practice and specialized trauma treatment — which is exactly InsideOut's positioning question. Use as calibration for how to describe the work InsideOut does without overclaiming clinical territory and without underclaiming the care it actually provides.

### transkulturellt-centrum-migration-psykisk-ohalsa-trauma-2018
*Region Stockholm, Transkulturellt Centrum.* Studiehäfte — *Migration, psykisk ohälsa och trauma*, 2018.
A knowledge-base document rather than a VP, but the authoritative Swedish-language framing of the interior of a displaced traumatized person in an institutional voice. Vocabulary reference for any technical passages the VP may need to tighten.

### raddningsmissionen-verksamhetsplan-2025
*Göteborgs Räddningsmission.* Verksamhetsplan 2025.
The closest structural peer for a Gothenburg-based VP at roughly InsideOut's order of magnitude (Räddningsmissionen is larger — ~250 staff across ~30 programs — but the civic context is identical). Short, plain-prose, organized around concrete yearly goals: *volontärstrategi, samverkan med huvudmän, opinionsbildning, nya verksamheter, stödsystem, effektmätning.* Writes institutionally without corporate inflation. Note: the PDF's text layer is thin (image-heavy layout), so skim the PDF directly rather than relying on the `.txt`. The strongest single *structural* reference for how a Gothenburg peer lays out a VP on the page.

### raddningsmissionen-arsredovisning-2024
*Göteborgs Räddningsmission.* Årsredovisning 2024.
Companion förvaltningsberättelse with program-by-program reporting (Solrosen, Agape, and others). Useful for seeing how a Gothenburg peer summarizes sub-programs at scale, and for the narrative-to-financials transition.

### goteborgs-stadsmission-arsredovisning-2024
*Stiftelsen Göteborgs Kyrkliga Stadsmission.* Årsredovisning 2024.
Distinct from Stockholms Stadsmission — this is the Gothenburg diaconal stiftelse. A conventional förvaltningsberättelse, included as the minimum institutional template a Gothenburg diaconal organization uses in its reporting toward Göteborgs Stad and other local funders. Useful mainly as a floor: this is what "correct and inoffensive" looks like in the local institutional register.

### kompis-sverige-arsredovisning-2024
*Kompis Sverige.* Årsredovisning 2024.
Small integration-focused ideell förening, IOP-partner, buddy-program method. The closest peer in *scale* to InsideOut: one core method, allmännyttig, written in a modest voice. The best model in the collection for how a small organization writes a formal årsredovisning without overclaiming and without reaching for Rädda Barnen's voice. Read before drafting any grant-facing section where the pull toward overclaim is strongest.

### maskrosbarn-verksamhetsberattelse-2023
*Maskrosbarn.* Verksamhetsberättelse 2023.
Maskrosbarn supports children of parents with addiction or mental illness — psychosocial, one target group, one coherent method set, mid-sized. Strong register reference for how to write about vulnerable children with warmth and institutional credibility side by side.

### maskrosbarn-arsberattelse-2024
*Maskrosbarn.* Årsberättelse 2024.
Most recent annual. Pairs with the 2023 VB to show voice continuity, and with the effektrapport below to show how the same year's work is narrated in two different document forms.

### maskrosbarn-effektrapport-2024
*Maskrosbarn.* Effektrapport 2024 (Giva Sverige format).
Particularly useful as a Giva-Sverige-format template at peer scale. Shows how an organization Maskrosbarn's size assembles a Giva-compliant effektrapport without either bureaucratizing the language or diluting the measurement. A practical model for the §6 measurement section of the VP if the board moves in that direction.

### bris-effektrapport-2024
*Bris — Barnens Rätt i Samhället.* Effektrapport (impact report), 2024.
The closest structural mirror in the collection for InsideOut's child-and-family work. Bris organizes around three verbs — *stödja, stärka, påverka* (support, strengthen, influence) — and five fokusområden running 2025–2029. It carries a Theory of Change diagram explicitly. The voice is careful and institutionally clean without being dead; useful as evidence that the grant register does not have to be hollow. Watch especially how they describe their own purpose in the opening (`Om BRIS` → `Syfte` → `Ändamål`).

### kvinna-till-kvinna-strategi-2023-2028
*The Kvinna till Kvinna Foundation.* Global strategy 2023–2028, "A push for lasting peace." English.
Closest in lineage to InsideOut's frontline humanitarian ground — feminist, conflict-context, partner-based work. The structure is worth studying: *Who we are* (values, vision, mandate), *Our journey*, *Global context*, *Our future direction* (theory of change, areas of change, avenues of contribution), *The way we work* (approaches, partnerships, knowledge, communication & advocacy), *Implementing the strategy*. Section 5.4 is a formal communications-and-advocacy section inside the strategy document itself — a direct model for how comms can be integrated rather than bolted on.

### kvinna-till-kvinna-arsrapport-2023
*The Kvinna till Kvinna Foundation.* Annual report 2023.
Pairs with the strategy above. Shows how the strategic frame turns into a narrated year.

### sak-verksamhetsberattelse-2024
*Svenska Afghanistankommittén.* Årsrapport + effektrapport 2024.
A 40+ year humanitarian organization whose 2024 was rupturing: SAK was forced to hand over operations to NAC and DACAAR and terminate Afghan staff after Taliban restrictions. The report is a masterclass in *writing a verksamhetsberättelse when your theory of change has been externally broken* — honest about what pausing in winter meant for comparability, open about monitoring-at-a-distance, transparent about *överlämning och avveckling*. For any organization whose context can shift under them (as InsideOut's can — the population it serves is shaped by a war still in progress), SAK's tone on *"förutsättningarna är väsentligt annorlunda än för tidigare rapporter"* is a template for writing honestly about conditions you do not control.

### fryshuset-verksamhetsberattelse-2024
*Fryshuset.* Årsrapport 2024. Verksamhetsberättelse with integrated effektrapport, hållbarhetsrapport, and årsredovisning in one document.
Fryshuset is useful for a specific reason: it is an organization that works with young people in a register that is warm and close to its community without becoming institutionally flabby. The integrated structure (impact + sustainability + financials in one document) is one answer to the problem of speaking in multiple registers at once — they are not separated, they are laid alongside each other. Opening line is worth reading: *"Meningsfulla sammanhang får människor att växa"* — the register InsideOut's public voice already lives close to.

### farr-verksamhetsberattelse-2025
*Flyktinggruppernas Riksråd (FARR).* Verksamhetsberättelse 2025–26.
A paraplyorganisation for asylum-rights groups, partipolitiskt och religiöst obunden. Sits squarely in the rights register — Tidöavtalet tracking, tonårsutvisningar, EU asyl- och migrationspakt, spårbyte, FOSIF/FOG/Asylforum. FARR makes a clean separation between *stöd*-work (rådgivning, förvarsbesök) and *påverkan*-work (Artikel 14, remissvar). The separation is a useful instrument: if InsideOut wants to name its work precisely, "we do one of these, not the other" is a clearer position than trying to hold both in one phrase.

### farr-verksamhetsplan-2026
*FARR.* Verksamhetsplan 2026 (utkast till årsmötet).
A notably thin ~75-line document. Worth reading specifically as a counter-example to InsideOut's VP 2026: FARR's VP is deliberately *invitational* — *"finns det tankar om annat FARR borde driva är det också av värde"*. It is a plan written as an opening move toward the membership, not as a finished artifact handed down from the board. The structural question for InsideOut — *are we writing a plan for a board or a plan for a movement* — is pulled into sharp focus by the contrast.

### radda-barnen-strategisk-vp-2025-2027
*Rädda Barnen.* Strategisk verksamhetsplan, 2025–2027. 49 pages, bilingual Swedish/English.
The most institutionally polished document in the set, and useful precisely because it is. Four focus areas for Sweden (mental health, school, freedom from violence, societal responsibility), a separate international section, a block of "enablers" (advocacy, digitalization, shifting power, funding), and financial conditions with budget. This is what a large, mature Swedish NGO's VP looks like when addressed to its own board. Useful as the far end of the spectrum — the register InsideOut's grant-facing documents answer to, not the register its public writing can stay in without suffocating.

### stockholms-stadsmission-arsredovisning-2024
*Stockholms Stadsmission.* Årsredovisning 2024.
A large urban social mission. Less of a direct shape match but useful as a reference for how humanitarian work with marginalized populations is reported in a Swedish institutional register that carries weight with funders and the public simultaneously.

### mind-and-life-europe-activity-2024
*Mind & Life Europe.* Activity Report 2024. 76 pages, English.
This is the find that most directly answers the gap in the "What this collection is missing" section below. Mind & Life Europe is the European sister of the Mind & Life Institute founded out of the 1987 Dalai Lama–Francisco Varela dialogues, funding research at the intersection of contemplative practice and science. The Activity Report is VP-shaped — core programmes, partnership events, programme development, members' projects, organisational development, full budget, board roster — and yet the opening frames MLE as "a home for unconventional interdisciplinary encounters" where "holding an open-hearted and interdisciplinary space of dialogue is in itself a radical, ethical mode of being-in-the-world." The contemplative register ("inner and systemic transformation," "collective sense-making with transformative potential," "contemplative wisdom traditions") sits on the same page as programme budgets and audit references without either collapsing. Closest single example in the collection of holding both the awakening register and the funder register at once. Pair with `mind-and-life-europe-activity-2023` for a second year of the same voice.

### mind-and-life-europe-activity-2023
*Mind & Life Europe.* Activity Report 2023. English.
Prior year of the same document. Useful for checking whether the register is stable across years (it is) and for seeing the "Care for Life" three-year arc at an earlier stage. Less load-bearing than the 2024 report; kept as confirmation that the voice is not a one-off.

### community-of-interbeing-uk-annual-2024
*Community of Interbeing UK.* Annual Report and Financial Statements, Year Ended 31 December 2024. UK charity 1096680.
The UK Plum Village Sangha — the closest thing to an institutional reporting document from the Thich Nhat Hanh lineage in the collection. Registered charity, full trustees' report + financial statements format. The "About" and "Annual Review" sections move fluently between the explicit practice register ("Mindfulness is the energy of being aware and awake to the present moment... practising mindfulness cultivates understanding, love, compassion, and joy") and the institutional armature (charitable aims, governance, Sangha groups supported, financial statements). Notable: the interior language is not softened for the Charity Commission audience — the trustees' report simply says what the practice is, in the lineage's own words, and lets the statutory machinery wrap around it. A good counterpoint to MiSP: where MiSP translates outward, COI-UK holds the source language and lets the form accommodate it. Shorter and less ambitious than MLE, but the instinct is the same.

### misp-impact-2020
*Mindfulness in Schools Project (MiSP).* Impact Report 2019/20. UK charity 1168992.
MiSP is a UK registered charity whose core work is teaching mindfulness curricula in schools. The Impact Report is addressed to funders, trustees and sector peers (chair's letter, beneficiary metrics, COVID response, financial summary). The handling of the tension is instructive but partial: MiSP mostly *resolves* the tension by translating the interior language into the safer institutional register of "wellbeing," "skills," "support" — the contemplative source is named in the framing and then handed off to evidence, training statistics, and educator testimony. Useful as a study in how an org at the mindfulness-education end of the spectrum chooses to fold the interior into the outcomes rather than hold both registers live on the page. This is one of the main ways the tension can go — worth reading for what InsideOut would lose if it took this route.

### suicide-zero-arsredovisning-2024
*Suicide Zero.* Verksamhetsberättelse och årsredovisning 2024.
Included as a structural reference for how a small-to-mid Swedish mental-health NGO structures a VP that must hold both public-facing tone and donor accountability. The register is advocacy-heavy rather than trauma-interior — useful for form, not for voice.

### im-effektrapport-2024
*Individuell Människohjälp (IM).* Effektrapport 2024.
A weaker match on the trauma-interior axis — IM's register is rights/power/organizing rather than interior work — but a solid example of Giva Sverige effektrapport structure from a peer-sized psychosocial-adjacent organization. Keep as structural reference only; do not use as a voice reference.

### kfum-central-verksamhetsberattelse-2024
*KFUM Central (Stockholm local förening).* Verksamhetsberättelse 2024.
A mid-size local KFUM förening rather than the national KFUM Sverige. Community/youth register. Useful as a *local KFUM* counterweight if the board wants to see how a single-location folk-movement organization writes about itself — and as a reminder that "local movement voice" and "grant voice" are not as far apart as they look on paper.

### scouterna-vp-2026
*Scouterna.* Verksamhetsplan 2026.
Included as a smaller, lighter VP — a folk-movement organization rather than a professionalized NGO. Useful as a contrast to Rädda Barnen and Kvinna till Kvinna: this is what a VP looks like when it speaks to a membership that is the organization, rather than to a board overseeing staff. The structural question for InsideOut — *are we writing a plan for a board or a plan for a movement* — is exactly what the contrast between this and Rädda Barnen reveals.

## communication/

Material specifically on communication, tone of voice, and the ethics of how an NGO speaks about the people it works with. Swedish guides for föreningskommunikation, two academic theses on strategic communication in nonprofits, and one ethical code that matters more than the rest combined.

### dochas-poster & concord-dochas-2014
*Dóchas (Irish association of development NGOs).* Code of Conduct on Images and Messages.
The single most load-bearing piece of material in the communications folder, and the closest thing to a lineage anchor for how an organization working with people who have suffered should speak about them. It is short. Read it first. The three principles are: respect for dignity, belief in equality, the need to promote fairness, solidarity and justice. The commitments that follow — truthfulness to context, avoidance of stereotyping/sensationalizing, informed consent of subjects, giving people the opportunity to tell their own stories — are exactly the discipline InsideOut's trauma-adjacent public voice has to operate under. This code was adopted in 2007 by Irish development NGOs and has since been taken up across the European humanitarian sector. It is the "how we speak about the people we work with, without betraying them" answer that has been tested in many rooms.

### rfsisu-effektiv-foreningskommunikation
*RF-SISU.* Effektiv föreningskommunikation. Lärgruppsmaterial (study-circle material), October 2022.
A practical Swedish guide to planning communication in an ideell förening. Structured as a study circle — designed to be worked through by a group, not read solo. The form itself is worth noticing: the act of building a communications framework through group conversation is the act insideout.md names as *"an act of collective self-understanding."* This material is a practical scaffold for exactly that conversation.

### foreningspool-planera-kommunikation-2025
*Föreningspool Malmö.* Planera föreningens kommunikation, 2025.
A compact planning guide distinguishing *kommunikationsstrategi* (what you want to say, to whom, where) from *kommunikationsplan* (how to execute) and *kriskommunikationsplan* (what happens when things go wrong). The distinction is clean and useful; InsideOut's current question is squarely at the strategy level, not the execution level, and this helps name the boundary.

### rum-kommunikationsstrategi-2018-2021
*Riksförbundet Unga Musikanter (RUM).* Kommunikationsstrategi 2018–2021.
A concrete example of a completed communications strategy document from a small-to-medium Swedish folk-movement organization. Useful not for its content — it will not match InsideOut's — but as *form reference*: what does the artifact actually look like on the page when someone has finished writing one?

### diva-kommunikationsarbete-ideella-org
*Kandidatuppsats, DiVA archive.* "Kommunikationsarbete i ideella organisationer."
Academic work on how nonprofits do communication in practice. Useful for the diagnostic it offers — the gap between stated values and actual communicative practice is a recurring theme — which is the exact register InsideOut's question is operating in.

### diva-strategisk-kommunikation-ideella
*Kandidatuppsats, DiVA archive.* "Strategisk kommunikation i ideella organisationer."
Companion academic piece focused on the strategic dimension. Heavier on theory; read the conclusions first and decide whether the framework sections are worth going back for.

## What this collection is missing

Naming the gaps honestly, because the gaps shape what the next pass has to find. Updated after the April 2026 second pass, which filled three of the four gaps that were previously named here.

- **InsideOut's direct Ukraine-adjacent peers.** *Partially addressed.* HUG's 2024 verksamhetsberättelse and its 127-page *Better You&Me* research report are now in the collection and are the closest peer voice available. FARR adds a rights-register counterpoint and SAK 2024 adds a template for writing honestly when context ruptures externally. What is still out of reach: Ukrainska Alliansen i Sverige, Beredskapslyftet (publishes quarterly but no consolidated VP/effektrapport), Caritas Sverige (only independent since 2026-01-01), and Rädda Barnen's Ukraine sub-report (folded into main strategy, no separate artifact). These may simply not exist as public PDFs.
- **Psychosocial / trauma-specific NGO voice.** *Addressed.* Svenska Röda Korsets *Hälsa och vård* årsbok (2021 and 2025) is now the primary vocabulary reference for describing the interior of traumatized displaced people in a Swedish institutional register. Transkulturellt Centrum's faktablad and 2018 studiehäfte add the technical vocabulary layer. The gap on this axis is effectively closed; any further pass should focus on *sentence-level* calibration between these sources and InsideOut's VP, not on gathering more.
- **The Dispenza-adjacent contemplative register in an institutional voice.** *Partially addressed.* Mind & Life Europe's activity reports (2023, 2024) are the clearest working example in the collection of an organization holding both the contemplative register and the funder register live on the same page — the voice is more Varela/phenomenological than Dispenza/quantum-mind, but it is unambiguously an interior register held institutionally without collapse. The Community of Interbeing UK annual report shows a different solution to the same tension: keep the lineage's own practice language in the trustees' report and let the statutory form wrap around it. MiSP's 2019/20 impact report shows the more common strategy, the *translate-outward* move where the interior source is named briefly and then handed to wellbeing/evidence language; useful as a warning of what InsideOut would lose by collapsing to the institutional side. What is still missing specifically: a Swedish-language document in this register, and anything closer to the explicit awakening / consciousness vocabulary InsideOut's own public posts use — that end of the spectrum still appears to live only in retreat-centre brochures and teacher-facing materials, not in institutional reporting. The Dispenza-proximate register may genuinely not exist in VP form anywhere public; InsideOut may have to do some of the finding itself.
- **Peer-sized Swedish NGO VPs at InsideOut's scale.** *Addressed.* Göteborgs Räddningsmission VP 2025 + Årsredovisning 2024 is the primary Gothenburg peer, Kompis Sverige is the closest small-allmännyttig peer by size, and Maskrosbarn's three documents cover VB + ÅB + Giva-effektrapport at mid-scale. The collection is no longer weighted only toward national giants.
- **Tone of voice as a stand-alone artifact.** *Still missing.* Most of the Swedish material treats communication as *planning* (what/where/when/how). The Dóchas code handles *ethics*. Missing: the tone-of-voice document as a separate artifact — the kind of internal guide that lets a group of writers speak with one mouth without being told what to say. International charity-sector examples exist (the Econsultancy/Charity Digital material surfaced in search) but were not captured as documents; a next pass could collect a few of those.
