# Reference alignment — reading the collection against the core

Intended as the opening section of the next VP 2026 review. Its job is to name, before anything else, where each reference stands in relation to InsideOut's five core values, so that the review session — and anyone reading the review later — knows which voices can be read for tone and which must be held at arm's length because borrowing from them would quietly pull the work off its inside.

The five values, as a shorthand for the pass below:

- **Healing begins inside.** The direction of work is inward first; the outside follows.
- **A person is already whole.** Nothing is installed. Nothing is repaired. The work makes space for what is already alive.
- **A person is a creator.** The person we meet is the one shaping what comes next — not a recipient, not a case.
- **Pain is a doorway.** Pain is honored as the opening into what was hidden beneath it, not as a problem to be solved or bypassed.
- **Inside and outside are one motion.** The personal and the civic are the same field at different scales.

The values are not a rubric. A document that reads as *not aligned* can still be load-bearing — as a form reference, as a vocabulary anchor, as a counter-example of the collapse this work is trying to avoid. The tags below are about *voice contamination*, not *usefulness*.

## Special status — the lineage, read honestly

Two documents from the HUG phase, read here in a way that distinguishes what InsideOut is carrying forward from what InsideOut left behind. The spin-out itself is the relevant fact: InsideOut exists because the interior work needed its own container that HUG's humanitarian frame was not shaped to hold. That split shapes how both of these documents should be read.

### hug-bym-research-evaluation-2023-2025
**InsideOut's own prior voice — with a qualifier.** Written by the people who are now InsideOut, during the HUG phase, before the spin-out. This is the closest available answer to *what does InsideOut already sound like when it writes institutionally about its own work*, and it is load-bearing for that reason.

But: some of BYM's register came from operating inside HUG's humanitarian container. A few of the compromises it made on framing, audience, and positioning were compromises the parent organization's shape required — not compromises InsideOut still has to make. The review should read BYM with two questions held at once: *what is already in our own voice from a year ago that belongs in the next document*, and *what did we write in that tone because HUG's frame was what we had, and what would we write differently now that we are our own container?* The answer to the second question is part of why the spin-out happened.

### hug-annual-report-2024
**The parent organization — and the path InsideOut did not take.** HUG's 2024 verksamhetsberättelse has two halves, and they are not one voice.

The **Ukraine-side humanitarian work** — 97 rescue vehicles, 40 MSEK medical aid, 5000 tourniquets — is classic humanitarian intervention register. People in acute need, goods and logistics delivered, deficit met. It is important and honorable work, and it is *not* InsideOut's path. It reads as clearly not aligned with the core on the same ground as the charity and clinical registers below: the subject is the organization delivering, the object is the population receiving, and the motion is outside-in by construction. Acute war-response *has* to run on that motion; it is not a criticism of HUG that its Ukraine work does.

The **Sweden-side psychosocial and integration work** — 2700 participants, 98% recommend rate, Nova Klubb, the Healing Beyond Fear line, what BYM was documenting — is aligned, and it is aligned because it *is* InsideOut. This half of HUG is the half that became its own organization. Reading the 2024 VB is useful precisely for seeing the tension between these two halves on one page: the humanitarian register and the inside-out register sharing an institutional container that was never quite the right shape for the second one. That tension is why the spin-out happened. Any review of InsideOut's 2026 VP should hold that context: InsideOut's voice has to do now what HUG's container could not fully hold — name its work without leaning on the humanitarian register as the default institutional voice around it.

## Clearly aligned — can be read for voice

Four references where the inside motion is held on the page rather than translated away. These are safe to read slowly and borrow from at the sentence level.

### fryshuset-verksamhetsberattelse-2024
Opens with *"Meningsfulla sammanhang får människor att växa"* and names its *värdegrund* in plain language: *"vårt arbete bygger på kärlek och en tro på människan. Vi arbetar alltid för något — inte emot."* The trust in the person — *tro på människan* — is the same claim InsideOut makes as *a person is already whole* and *a person is a creator*. Holds warmth next to a 570 MSEK hållbarhetsrapport without either collapsing. The closest existing Swedish institutional example of what InsideOut is reaching for.

### kvinna-till-kvinna-strategi-2023-2028 & kvinna-till-kvinna-arsrapport-2023
Partner-based rather than beneficiary-based: the people the organization works with are named as partners shaping the work, not as recipients of it. That framing is aligned with *a person is a creator* at the structural level — it is the one reference in the collection that has built its entire theory of change on agency rather than need.

### mind-and-life-europe-activity-2024 & mind-and-life-europe-activity-2023
Holds the interior register ("inner and systemic transformation," "contemplative wisdom traditions," "open-hearted interdisciplinary space") live on the same page as programme budgets, board rosters, and audit references. Closest example in the collection of the two-register question InsideOut is actually facing. The specific vocabulary is Varela/phenomenological rather than InsideOut's own register — but the *instinct* (that the two registers can share a page without betraying each other) is the instinct to borrow.

### community-of-interbeing-uk-annual-2024
Different solution to the same problem as MLE. Where MLE finds a shared vocabulary for both registers, COI-UK keeps the lineage's own practice language in the trustees' report and lets the UK Charity Commission's statutory form wrap around it. Worth reading for the confidence of *not translating* the interior language out for the funder audience.

## Clearly not aligned — read for form, not for voice

These are the references the review should explicitly flag. They are useful, sometimes necessary, as structural templates or as vocabulary sources — but their *voice* would pull InsideOut's work off its inside if borrowed directly. Naming them upfront prevents that quiet leak.

### Clinical/treatment register — people as patients

**roda-korset-halsa-vard-arsbok-2025**, **roda-korset-halsa-vard-arsbok-2021**, **transkulturellt-centrum-psykosocialt-stod-faktablad**, **transkulturellt-centrum-migration-psykisk-ohalsa-trauma-2018**

These are the Swedish institutional benchmark for describing the interior of traumatized displaced people — and that is exactly why they must be handled carefully. They operate from a treatment model: a patient arrives with a condition, a clinician meets them, something is diagnosed, something is treated. That model *directly contradicts* two of InsideOut's core values. *A person is already whole* is not a claim a treatment centre can make without undermining its own work — if the person is already whole, there is nothing to treat. *A person is a creator* is also not available in a clinical room, where the person is by definition receiving care rather than shaping it.

**How to use them anyway.** These documents are the vocabulary source for how to name what people are carrying — *trauma, tortyr, flykt, anknytning* — in a Swedish institutional register without clinical distance. Borrow the *words* about the weight, not the *frame* about who the person is. Any sentence where the frame leaks (*"patienter", "behandling", "åtgärder"*) is a sentence to rewrite.

### Charity / diaconal register — people as recipients

**raddningsmissionen-verksamhetsplan-2025**, **raddningsmissionen-arsredovisning-2024**, **goteborgs-stadsmission-arsredovisning-2024**, **stockholms-stadsmission-arsredovisning-2024**

The diaconal tradition is explicitly about meeting vulnerability with care — which is honorable work, but it positions the person met as *utsatt* (exposed, vulnerable) and the organization as the one that helps. That is the inverse of *a person is a creator*: the structural subject of a diaconal document is the giver, and the object is the receiver. Use these for the local Gothenburg *structure* of a VP — Räddningsmissionen VP 2025 in particular is the strongest existing template for how a Gothenburg org lays out its yearly plan — but do not let the recipient/giver polarity leak into InsideOut's own framing.

### Rights / advocacy register — pain as injustice to fight, not doorway to honor

**farr-verksamhetsberattelse-2025**, **farr-verksamhetsplan-2026**, **bris-effektrapport-2024**, **suicide-zero-arsredovisning-2024**, **im-effektrapport-2024**

The rights register is organized around campaigning, advocacy, and political action — which is important work, but it reads pain as *injustice* (something to be fought externally) rather than as *doorway* (something to be honored interiorly). This is the clearest misalignment with InsideOut's *pain is a doorway*. A VP that borrows this register will find itself writing sentences like *"vi kämpar mot..."* where InsideOut would need to write *"vi möter..."*. Bris is the most *structurally* useful of this group (the *stödja/stärka/påverka* verbs, the Theory of Change diagram) — take the structure; reread the voice through the value before adopting any of it.

### Institutional mandate-derived — derived from external authority rather than interior

**radda-barnen-strategisk-vp-2025-2027**

Rädda Barnen's entire framing rests on Barnrättsagendan — the child rights convention as the source from which the work derives. That is a coherent and legitimate theory of change for Rädda Barnen, but it is fundamentally an *outside-in* move: the authority comes from a document ratified by states, and the organization executes on behalf of that authority. InsideOut's whole orientation is inside-out — the authority for the work comes from what is alive in the person, not from what has been ratified about them. Use Rädda Barnen as the far end of the institutional spectrum, the register grant-facing writing answers to — but any sentence that starts "våra insatser utgår från..." should be tested against the inside before being kept.

### Translate-outward collapse — the exact move InsideOut is trying not to make

**misp-impact-2020**

MiSP is load-bearing specifically as a warning. The interior source (mindfulness as a contemplative practice) is named in the framing and then handed off to the safer institutional register of *wellbeing, skills, support, evidence*. The inside-out motion is translated into outside-in outcomes before it reaches the page. This is one of the two routes out of the voice tension InsideOut faces, and it is the route that would cost the most. Read it as a mirror of what collapse looks like, not as a template.

## Structurally useful, voice to approach carefully

References that sit in the middle — neither clearly aligned nor clearly misaligned. Useful at scale, form, or vocabulary level; the voice should be tested against the inside before borrowing.

### maskrosbarn-verksamhetsberattelse-2023, maskrosbarn-arsberattelse-2024, maskrosbarn-effektrapport-2024
Supports children whose parents have addiction or mental illness. The method honors the child's own voice — children co-author, shape, and lead parts of the work — which is aligned with *a person is a creator* at the practice level. But the framing of the children themselves still sits partly in a "vulnerable group to be supported" register. Read for the peer-support model and the Giva-Sverige effektrapport template; re-test any sentence that slips into *målgrupp*-language.

### kompis-sverige-arsredovisning-2024
Small integration-focused ideell förening at roughly InsideOut's scale. Buddy program — relationship-based rather than service-delivery-based, which is a gentler register than the charity documents above. The closest peer in *scale*. Safe to read for how a small organization writes a formal årsredovisning without overclaiming, though the voice is modest rather than alive.

### sak-verksamhetsberattelse-2024
A development organization's deficit/intervention register would normally be tagged as not aligned. What puts SAK here instead is the *tone of honesty* in its 2024 report — the way it writes about a theory of change that has been externally broken by the Taliban restrictions. That honesty is directly aligned with InsideOut's own *distinguish thought from truth*. Read for *how* it acknowledges what it does not control, not for *what* the organization does.

### scouterna-vp-2026, kfum-central-verksamhetsberattelse-2024
Folk-movement / community register. The membership *is* the organization — which has some resonance with *a person is a creator* at the civic scale. Useful as contrast documents for the structural question: is InsideOut writing a plan for a board or a plan for a movement? Voice-wise, neither is close enough to borrow sentences from.

## The shape of this pass, held honestly

Two things to name about the pass itself before it becomes part of a review:

First, the tagging above is a *reading*, not a ranking. A document tagged "clearly not aligned" is not a bad document — it is a document whose inside runs on a different motion than InsideOut's, and the tag is a note to the writer, not a judgment on the organization. Bris, Rädda Barnen, Röda Korset, Räddningsmissionen, Suicide Zero, and FARR are all doing real and important work in their own registers.

Second, the tag is about *voice leakage*, which is a subtle thing. A reviewer can read any of these references carefully without absorbing their register, *if they know in advance where the register runs counter to the inside*. The whole point of doing this pass before the review rather than during it is that during the review, the pull of structural elegance is strong enough that it can carry voice into the document underneath it. Naming the misalignments upfront lets the review borrow structure without borrowing frame.

Use this as the opening of the next review. The review itself can then get on with the work of the feedback pass without having to re-argue this ground at every step.
