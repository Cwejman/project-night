# InsideOut

This is what the work answers to — the felt shape of what cannot be lost. When these feel alive in the work, they are doing their job. When they start to feel like something we are applying, we have drifted, and the return is the work.

---

## Healing begins inside

What we hold inside shapes what comes out of us. What we cannot meet in ourselves returns to us from outside wearing another face. The direction the work moves is inward first — and everything else follows.

## A person is already whole

What someone needs is already in them, covered over by what they had to do to survive. Nothing is installed. Nothing is repaired. The work is to make the space in which what is already alive can come forward.

## A person is a creator

Not a problem to solve, not a condition to wait out — the person we meet is the one shaping what comes next, in their own life and in the life we share. Whatever we offer, we offer to that shaping.

## Pain is a doorway

The pain is real, heavy, sometimes unbearable — and also holy. It asks to be bowed to, not crossed. Honored fully enough to be felt, it opens into what was hidden beneath it. Pain bypassed is not met.

## Inside and outside are one motion

What happens inside a person becomes the world they walk through. What happens between people becomes the world we share. The personal and the civic are the same field at different scales, and losing either side loses both.

---

## The shape the work takes

The values take shape at three scales of human life: **Next Generation**, the child and the family; **Personal Evolution**, the self as it meets itself; **Connected Society**, the life between people. These are not three programs. They are one motion running through three scales of a human being, and the three circle each other more than they follow each other.

---

These are not rules. They are what the work answers to. If a decision, a project, or a word touches one of them, it belongs. If it does not, it is unanchored — and unanchored things drift.

InsideOut has a juridical form because Swedish law requires one, and the form is real — it protects what is arranged inside it. But the form does not generate the arrangement. The values organize. What belongs to InsideOut is what answers to them.
